# Decision Skill

## Purpose
Helps an AI agent compare alternatives under uncertainty. Designed to support human judgment, not replace it.

## When to Use
Choosing between business ideas, SaaS concepts, automation opportunities, marketing strategies, hiring decisions, product priorities, investment of time/money, operational changes.

## Decision Framework
Evaluate each option by: Impact, Cost, Risk, Speed, Complexity, Reversibility, Learning potential, Evidence available, Human review needed, Next test.

## Output Format
Decision:
Options:
Best Conditional Option:
Why:
Main Risk:
When Not to Use:
What Would Change the Conclusion:
Next Test:
Success Metric:

## Core Principle
A good decision under uncertainty is not the one that looks perfect. It is the one that reduces risk, creates learning, preserves reversibility, exposes assumptions, and can be tested quickly.

## Example
Decision: Should I build a full SaaS or start with a service?
Best Conditional Option: Start with a paid diagnostic service before building the SaaS.
Why: Validates pain, buyer, pricing and delivery before technical investment.
Main Risk: Service may not scale if not documented and standardized.
Next Test: Sell 3 paid diagnostics in 14 days.
Success Metric: At least 3 business owners pay for diagnosis and request implementation.

## Safety Rules
- Do not decide for the user.
- Do not create false certainty.
- Do not use numbers without assumptions.
- Do not ignore reversibility or cost of error.

## Final Principle
The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty. The goal is to make uncertainty visible enough for better action.
